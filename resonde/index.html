Purpose

Twice a day, every day, a weather balloon launches from each of the 104 weather stations all over the United States. After 2 hours of data acquisition, they turn into floating garbage bags and a shoe-box full of electronics. They burst around 100k ft, fall back to Earth as trash, and 80% are simply forgotten about. It's time for a smarter, sustainable solution.

Cost

The reSonde project will save government high altitude weather acquisition programs over $12 Million a year in recurring costs. This means a real and measurable way to cut wasteful spending, and apply those funds to something beneficial - like buying every resident in Oregon a $3 latte.

View details »

Capability

Temperature, winds, humidity. This is what gets collected today from the Earth's upper atmosphere. It feeds the airline industry, helps predict weather patterns, and aids atmospheric research. With a reusable vehicle, atmospheric sensors can grow in capability without fear of losing costly equipment. Everyone wins here.
